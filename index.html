My selection for the week

The Chilean version of Sopaipillas is a type of fried, round dough, and my favorite kind includes pumpkin—a choice that can spark debate even among Chileans! Sopaipillas can be enjoyed with avocado or pebre sauce for a savory twist, or in a sweet version with a chancaca syrup, known as sopaipillas pasadas

Another beloved Chilean dish is empanadas fritas, or fried empanadas. They can be filled with meat, seafood, or, as in this recipe, a delicious combination of cheese and shrimp. This is my favorite recipe, tied to countless holiday memories from my childhood.

Pasta is one of the best dishes Italy has given to the world. Today, it’s a global culinary favorite, and we can always add our own twist to each dish. This is my favorite recipe, made with asparagus, shrimp, and vegan cream.

Here’s another classic paella! This is a variant on the traditional recipe, but it’s just as delicious and has my own personal twist that I’d love to share today. The rice I cooked for my friends included chorizo, chicken, and shrimp—a true flavor explosion.

Lübeck, my dream city in Germany. Lübeck is a northern German city distinguished by Brick Gothic architecture, which dates to its time as the medieval capital of the Hanseatic League, a powerful trading confederation.

The hanseatic city of Lübeck takes its place at the Baltic sea and is not only known for being the hometown for the German writer Thomas Mann but also for its beautiful old town which is part of the UNESCO world cultural heritage. After Lübeck your next stop is Travemünde a unique city with historic buildings and a long beach that shakes up holiday flair.
